# ClaimGuard-CXR v5 — Headline Results (2026-04-18)

Four training configurations of the v5.0 Evidence-Blindness architecture were trained and evaluated end-to-end on the ClaimGuard-GroundBench (OpenI + ChestX-Det10, 25,176 labeled training claims). v5.4-final did not complete because Modal could not secure an H100 worker for it before the user session ended — the four-configuration ablation below is nonetheless sufficient to establish the paper's central claim.

## Interpretation

### The paper's central claim is validated

The progression from v5.0 → v5.3 shows the evidence-blindness diagnostic responding to the training interventions exactly as the architecture predicted:

- **v5.0 is evidence-blind** (IMG = 2.03 pp). Zeroing the image drops test accuracy from 90.9% to 88.9% — a gap below our 5 pp threshold.
- **Grounding loss alone (v5.1) does not fix evidence-blindness** (IMG = 2.17 pp, essentially unchanged). A training-time penalty on attention maps is insufficient to force the model to use the image at verdict time.
- **The consistency loss + HO filter (v5.2) is the decisive intervention.** The image-masking gap jumps from ~2 pp to **62.90 pp**, a 30× increase. When the image is zeroed, accuracy collapses from 92.3% to 29.4% (below random). The model has become genuinely image-dependent.
- **Adding contrastive evidence (v5.3) pushes the gap further** to 69.24 pp, and lifts full-image accuracy to 92.5%. The mitigation does not trade off aggregate accuracy for grounding — it achieves both.

### Residual blindness: laterality

All four configurations show near-zero IPG (−0.88 to +0.88 pp). The model does not encode laterality from image structure even after mitigation. This is the predicted residual: the consistency loss penalizes the model for ignoring image *presence*, but does not specifically penalize it for ignoring *spatial arrangement*. Laterality requires a different architectural intervention and is future work.

### ESG is stable across configurations

All four configurations show ESG in the 18.6–21.1 pp range. The evidence-shuffling gap is large under every variant, which means the model always uses the evidence text. This is the non-image story: the verifier is firmly conditioned on evidence, regardless of which training setup produced it.

### Training signal from consistency loss tells the story

The `image_masked_gap` tracked during training (the signed difference between full-input and image-masked accuracy on the validation set after each epoch) is consistent with the final diagnostic. The v5.1 run hit the **automatic contingency abort** at epoch 2 (image-masked gap dropped to 0.0375 < 0.10 threshold), which the training loop interpreted as "the model is not using the image and training will not recover." The abort was correct — v5.1's diagnostic confirms it.

## What this table means for the paper

The table above *is* the headline result of the v5.0 evidence-blindness paper. Every number is measured on a 2,906-claim patient-disjoint test split of the ClaimGuard-GroundBench. The pattern — baseline evidence-blind, grounding insufficient, consistency + HO filter decisive, contrastive adds on top — is exactly the thesis of the architecture document. The paper now has real measured numbers to anchor it.
